Self-Driving Cars

  • Self-driving cars (also known as autonomous vehicles) are vehicles that can navigate and drive without human input, using advanced software and sensors.
  • There are six levels of driving automation defined by industry standards, from Level 0 (fully manual) to Level 5 (fully autonomous with no human intervention)​:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.
  • These cars rely on sensors (cameras, radar, lidar) and AI algorithms to perceive their surroundings and make driving decisions in real time​:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.
  • Key potential benefits include increased road safety, reduced traffic congestion, and improved mobility for people who cannot drive​:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}​:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
